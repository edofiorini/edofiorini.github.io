I am a Research Scientist in the **Interactive Skill Learning (ISL)** group, within the Cognitive Robotics (KRO) department and the Future Manufacturing domain, at the **DLR Institute of Robotics and Mechatronics** (German Aerospace Center), in Oberpfaffenhofen, Munich.

My research focuses on injecting human priors into learning models to enable interactive learning of generated robotic skills, bridging the gap at the intersection of machine learning and control in robotics. Specifically, I work on:

* Methods to detect human-robot physical interaction and provide corrective feedback using uncertainty-aware energy tanks, reducing the gap between learned models and safe physical execution.
* User-friendly interfaces that allow users to segment trajectories, enabling the specification of task-relevant priors for individual regions of a task.
* Reinforcement Learning from Imitation Learning with human-in-the-loop, leveraging physical human feedback during exploration to accelerate the learning process.
* Diffusion policies and Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models with human-in-the-loop input, for continuous skill adaptation.

I am involved in the EU project **INVERSE** (INteractive robots that intuitiVely lEarn to inVErt tasks by ReaSoning about their Execution), applying research on human-robot collaboration to enable robots to intuitively learn how to invert a skill, validated on two real-world use cases provided by Kone Cranes and Centro Ricerche Fiat (CRF).

I've also had the chance to showcase this work live at international fairs such as Automatica, demonstrating real-time problem-solving on real robots for tasks such as cable insertion and bearing ring insertion.

Background
======
I graduated with a Master's Degree in Computer Engineering for Robotics and Smart Industry from the University of Verona, Italy (110/110 cum laude), after a Bachelor's Degree in Bioinformatics from the same university. My academic background combines Programming, Robotics, Control Theory, Computer Vision, and Machine Learning, with a strong interest in investigating how these fields can be merged to build intelligent systems.

Before joining DLR, I worked as a Robotics Researcher at the ALTAIR Robotics Lab (University of Verona), where I developed motion planning and learning-by-demonstration methods for both agricultural robotics (an autonomous rover for precision agriculture) and collaborative welding robotics (my Master's thesis, later published at CASE 2024). I also spent a research period at DLR's Institute of Robotics and Mechatronics within the Factory of the Future (FoF) team, contributing to a flexible production network demo shown at the Automatica trade fair.

My areas of interest include Industrial Robotics, Control and Systems Theory, Anomaly Detection, Learning by Demonstration, and Teleoperation, and I'm always curious to extend my skills into new fields such as surgical and agricultural robotics.

Outside the lab, you'll find me at music festivals, hiking in the mountains, or chasing unforgettable views through my camera lens.

Feel free to reach out if you'd like to talk about robotics, human-robot collaboration, or interactive learning!
