Built the Industrial Robot Anomaly Detection (IndRAD) dataset as a benchmark for evaluating fault detection algorithms on industrial robots. The dataset is composed of 13 nominal trajectories and 3 trajectories with structural anomalies, acquired on a KUKA LBR iiwa robot. The work includes a protocol for injecting sensory anomalies into clean data, and an evaluation of three anomaly detection methods from the literature: PCA-based detection, deep autoencoders, and one-class classification. All code was developed in Python.
