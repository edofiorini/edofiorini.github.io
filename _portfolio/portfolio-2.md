Implementation and validation of a robust motion planning workflow for a welding robot based on the Learning by Demonstration paradigm, through a hand-tool pointer detected by an optical system and the welding workpiece CAD model.

* Developed a system enabling the robot to learn welding trajectories from human demonstrations, captured via a hand-held tool pointer tracked by an optical motion capture system and referenced against the workpiece's CAD model
* Built and validated an experimental collaborative welding cell integrating a Franka Emika Panda robotic manipulator with an OptiTrack motion capture system, developing the full software stack in C++ and ROS
* Research findings were published at CASE 2024 (IEEE International Conference on Automation Science and Engineering): ["Clothoid-based CAD Model Compensation for Precise Welding in Manufacturing Processes"](https://iris.univr.it/bitstream/11562/1166127/1/Clothoid-based_CAD_Model_Compensation_for_Precise_Welding_in_Manufacturing_Processes.pdf)

Carried out as my Master's thesis at the ALTAIR Robotics Lab, University of Verona, under the supervision of Prof. Riccardo Muradore.
